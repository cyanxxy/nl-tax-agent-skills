## Purpose

This reference describes how to apply box 1 income rules when preparing the annual return 2025 workpack. It covers income classification, source document mapping, and common edge cases.

These are reference notes for workpack preparation -- not final tax advice.

---

## Income components in box 1

Box 1 covers income from work and home (inkomen uit werk en woning). The following components are included:

### Employment income (loon uit dienstbetrekking)

- Gross salary from the jaaropgaaf minus employee insurance premiums equals the taxable wage (belastbaar loon)
- The jaaropgaaf field "loon voor de loonheffing" is the starting point for the return
- "Ingehouden loonheffing" is the wage tax already withheld by the employer -- this is credited against the final tax liability
- "Premie Zvw" (Zorgverzekeringswet contribution) is withheld separately and is not deductible by the employee

### Pension income (pensioenuitkeringen)

- Pension benefits received during 2025 are added to box 1
- Source documents: pensioenoverzicht, UPO (Uniform Pensioenoverzicht)
- AOW pension received from SVB is also box 1 income
- Withheld loonheffing on pension income is credited against the final tax liability

### Benefit income (uitkeringen)

- Social benefits from UWV (WW, WIA, WAO, Ziektewet) are box 1 income
- SVB benefits (AOW, Anw) are box 1 income
- Municipal benefits (bijstand) are box 1 income
- Source documents: uitkeringsspecificatie, jaaropgaaf from UWV/SVB
- Withheld loonheffing on benefits is credited against the final tax liability

## Key fields from the jaaropgaaf

When processing a jaaropgaaf, extract and use these fields:

| Field | Dutch name | Use in return |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Taxable wage | Loon voor de loonheffing | Added to box 1 taxable income |
| Wage tax withheld | Ingehouden loonheffing | Credited against final tax |
| Health insurance contribution | Premie Zvw (inkomensafhankelijke bijdrage) | NOT deductible; informational only |
| Employment discount basis | Verrekende arbeidskorting | Used to verify arbeidskorting |

## Multiple employers or income sources

When a taxpayer has multiple employers, pensions, or benefits in a single year:

1. Collect ALL jaaropgaven (one per employer/source)
2. Sum all "loon voor de loonheffing" amounts for total box 1 income
3. Sum all "ingehouden loonheffing" amounts for total wage tax credit
4. Apply the progressive tax brackets to the COMBINED total income
5. The combined income may push the taxpayer into a higher bracket, resulting in additional tax owed beyond what individual employers withheld
6. Each source withholds loonheffing independently, so the aggregate withholding may be insufficient for the combined income level

Flag a review question when multiple income sources are detected, as the taxpayer may owe additional tax.

## Common edge cases

### Company car (auto van de zaak / bijtelling)

- If the employer provides a company car for private use, a percentage of the catalogue value (cataloguswaarde) is added to the taxable wage
- The bijtelling percentage depends on the CO2 emissions of the vehicle (0%, 16%, or 22% for 2025)
- This amount is normally already included in the jaaropgaaf "loon voor de loonheffing"
- If the taxpayer drove fewer than 500 private kilometres, they may have a "verklaring geen privégebruik" and the bijtelling should not apply
- If the bijtelling seems incorrect or missing, flag it as a review question

### Stock options and equity compensation

- Stock options exercised during 2025 are taxable at the moment of exercise
- The taxable amount is the difference between the fair market value at exercise and the exercise price
- This income is normally included in the jaaropgaaf but may be reported on a separate statement
- RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) are taxed at vesting
- If the employer is foreign and did not withhold Dutch loonheffing, the income must still be reported in box 1

### Severance payments (ontslagvergoeding / transitievergoeding)

- Severance payments are box 1 income
- If paid out as a lump sum, the full amount is taxable in the year received
- If paid into a stamrecht (transitional arrangement, pre-2014), different rules may apply
- Large severance payments may push the taxpayer into a higher bracket -- flag for review

## Out of v1 scope

The following income types are part of box 1 but are NOT covered in v1 of this skill:

- **Self-employment income (winst uit onderneming):** entrepreneurs with an IB-onderneming are out of scope. If evidence suggests self-employment (e.g., winstaangifte, facturen), flag the case as potentially out of scope and suggest the taxpayer consult a tax adviser.
- **Income from other activities (resultaat uit overige werkzaamheden):** freelance or side income outside an employment relationship. Flag if detected.
- **Periodic payments received (alimentatie ontvangen):** alimony received from a former partner may be box 1 income. Flag if detected.
- **Foreign employment income:** income earned in another country with cross-border implications. Flag if detected.

When any of these are detected, add a note to `workspace/shared/review-questions.md` stating that the case may require specialist support.

## Notes

- Always verify that the tax year on the jaaropgaaf matches 2025.
- If the jaaropgaaf shows a tax year other than 2025, do not include it in the annual 2025 return. Flag it as an anomaly.
- The ingehouden loonheffing is a CREDIT (reduces tax owed), not additional income.
- For AOW-age taxpayers, the box 1 rate in schijf 1 is lower (17.92% instead of 35.82%) because AOW premie is not owed. See `box1-rates.md`.
