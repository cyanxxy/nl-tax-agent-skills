## Rule

A voorlopige aanslag should be reviewed whenever the taxpayer's financial situation changes significantly during the year. Failure to review and correct an inaccurate provisional assessment can lead to unexpected tax bills or required repayments when the annual return is filed.

## Situations requiring review

The following situations mean the voorlopige aanslag should be checked and potentially changed:

### Income changes

- Started a new job or changed employer
- Received a salary increase or decrease
- Lost employment (ontslag, einde contract)
- Retired or started receiving pension/AOW
- Started or stopped receiving benefits (WW, WIA, bijstand)
- Started or stopped self-employment

### Housing changes

- Bought a new home (new mortgage interest deduction)
- Sold a home (mortgage deduction may no longer apply)
- Moved to a different home
- Refinanced or changed mortgage terms
- Paid off mortgage or made extra mortgage repayments

### Partner situation changes

- Marriage or registered partnership (geregistreerd partnerschap)
- Separation or divorce
- Partner started or stopped working
- Death of a partner

### Deduction changes

- Started or stopped paying alimony (alimentatie)
- Changed insurance premiums (lijfrente, arbeidsongeschiktheid)
- Significant change in deductible care costs or gifts
- Changed charitable giving patterns

### Prior-year based assessment

- Received an automatically generated voorlopige aanslag based on prior-year data that no longer reflects the current situation
- The Belastingdienst may issue an EVA (Eerste Voorlopige Aanslag) based on the prior year -- if your situation has changed, this must be reviewed

## How to check

1. Obtain the beschikking (decision letter) for the current voorlopige aanslag
2. Compare the amounts listed on the beschikking with your actual current situation
3. Check each category: income, deductions, box 3 assets/debts
4. If any amounts are significantly different from your current reality, the voorlopige aanslag should be changed

## If amounts are wrong

- Follow the change procedure described in change-flow.md
- Remember: when changing, ALL data must be re-entered

## Risk of incorrect voorlopige aanslag

- If you receive too much refund (teruggaaf): you may have to repay the excess when filing the annual return
- If you pay too little: a large additional payment may be due at annual return time
- If you pay too much: you will receive the overpayment back, but your money is tied up unnecessarily

## Developer instruction

When helping a user review their voorlopige aanslag:

1. Ask about life events and changes since the voorlopige aanslag was issued or last changed
2. Walk through each category (income, deductions, box 3) and compare to the amounts on the beschikking
3. If discrepancies are found, recommend changing the voorlopige aanslag and guide to change-flow.md
4. If the user receives a monthly refund that may be too high, warn about repayment risk
5. If the assessment was automatically generated (EVA), explicitly ask whether the prior-year basis still applies

## Common failure

Do not assume that an existing voorlopige aanslag is correct just because the Belastingdienst issued it. Automatically generated assessments (EVA) are based on prior-year data and may be outdated. Always prompt the user to verify against their current situation.
